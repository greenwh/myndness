## Crisis Tools

**When to use**: During moments of high anxiety or distress

### Quick Access

From the dashboard, tap the red **"I need help now"** button to enter the crisis flow.

### 4-7-8 Breathing

**Purpose**: Quickly calm your nervous system

**How to use**:
1. The circle will guide you through the breathing pattern
2. **Inhale** for 4 seconds (circle grows)
3. **Hold** for 7 seconds (circle pauses)
4. **Exhale** for 8 seconds (circle shrinks)
5. Complete 4 cycles minimum (1-2 minutes)

**Why it works**: This breathing pattern activates your parasympathetic nervous system, telling your body it's safe.

### 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

**Purpose**: Reconnect with your present surroundings during anxiety or dissociation

**How to use**:
1. **5 things you can see** - Look around and name them
2. **4 things you can touch** - Feel textures around you
3. **3 things you can hear** - Listen to sounds near and far
4. **2 things you can smell** - Notice any scents
5. **1 thing you can taste** - What taste is in your mouth?

**Why it works**: Engages your five senses to anchor you in the present moment.

**When**: Track 1-3 times daily, especially during mood changes

**How to log**:
1. Go to **Track â†’ Mood**
2. **Rate mood**: 1 (very low) to 10 (very high)

6. **Save** - Your entry is logged with timestamp

**Optional**: Link to a blood pressure reading if anxiety affected your BP

### Blood Pressure Log

**When**: As directed by your healthcare provider (typically 2x daily)

**How to log**:
1. Go to **Track â†’ Blood Pressure**
2. Enter **systolic** (top number, 50-250 range)
3. Enter **diastolic** (bottom number, 30-150 range)
4. Add optional **notes** (e.g., "after morning coffee")
5. **Save** - Reading is timestamped

**Why track BP**: Anxiety can cause BP spikes. Tracking helps identify patterns and monitor cardiac health.

## CBT Tools

### Thought Records

**Purpose**: Challenge unhelpful thinking patterns using cognitive restructuring

**When to use**: When you notice distressing thoughts or strong emotions

**7-Step Process**:

1. **Situation**: What happened? (When, where, who)
   - Be specific: "During video call with my doctor at 2pm"

2. **Automatic Thought**: What went through your mind?
   - The first thought that popped up: "I'm going to say something stupid"

3. **Emotion**: What did you feel?
   - Select emotion and rate intensity (0-100)
   - Can rate multiple emotions

4. **Distortions**: What thinking traps are present?
   - Select from list of cognitive distortions
   - Reference guide available in CBT hub

5. **Evidence For & Against**:
   - **For**: Facts supporting the thought
   - **Against**: Facts contradicting the thought

6. **Balanced Thought**: Create a more realistic perspective
   - Consider all evidence
   - What would you tell a friend?

7. **Outcome**: How do you feel now?
   - Rate emotion intensity after this exercise
   - Notice the change

**Auto-save**: Your progress saves automatically every 500ms. You can exit and return anytime.

**View history**: Tools â†’ CBT â†’ View History
- See all completed records
- Resume incomplete drafts
- Filter by date range

### Cognitive Distortions Reference

**Purpose**: Learn to recognize thinking traps

**Common distortions**:
- **All-or-Nothing**: Seeing things as black and white
- **Overgeneralization**: One event means it always happens
- **Mental Filter**: Focusing only on negatives
- **Mind Reading**: Assuming you know what others think
- **Fortune Telling**: Predicting negative outcomes
- **Catastrophizing**: Expecting the worst-case scenario
- **Emotional Reasoning**: "I feel it, so it must be true"
- **Should Statements**: Rigid rules about how things "should" be
- **Labeling**: Defining yourself by one characteristic
- **Personalization**: Blaming yourself for things outside your control

**How to access**: Tools â†’ CBT â†’ Thinking Traps Guide

### Behavioral Experiments

**Purpose**: Test anxiety-provoking beliefs with real-world evidence

**When to use**: When you have a specific belief you want to challenge

**4-Step Setup**:

1. **Identify Belief**: What do you believe will happen?
   - Example: "If I call my doctor, I'll sound stupid"
   - Rate belief strength: 0-100

2. **Design Experiment**: What will you do to test this?
   - Example: "Call doctor's office to schedule appointment"
   - Be specific and safe

3. **Make Prediction**: What do you think will happen?
   - Example: "I'll stumble over words and they'll think I'm incompetent"
   - Rate confidence: 0-100

4. **Schedule** (optional): When will you do this?
   - Set a date
   - Link to a planned activity if relevant

**After Completing**:
1. Open incomplete experiment
2. **Record what actually happened**
3. **Note what you learned**
4. **Re-rate belief strength** - Notice if it changed
5. View before â†’ after comparison

**Why it works**: Direct evidence is more powerful than thought alone. Most predictions turn out to be inaccurate.

### Anxiety Hierarchy (Exposure Therapy)

**Purpose**: Gradually face fears through systematic desensitization

**When to use**: For persistent fears or phobias you want to overcome

**Getting Started**:

**Step 1: Build Your Ladder** (lowest to highest anxiety)

1. Go to Tools â†’ CBT â†’ Anxiety Hierarchy
2. Click **"Add New Fear"**
3. **Describe situation**: Be specific
4. **Category** (optional): Social, Health, Performance, etc.
5. **Rate current distress**: SUDS 0-100
   - SUDS = Subjective Units of Distress Scale
   - 0 = No distress at all
   - 50 = Moderate distress
   - 100 = Extreme distress
6. **Set target**: How low do you want to get?
   - Suggested: 30 points below current
   - Don't need to reach 0 - just manageable

**Step 2: Face Each Fear Repeatedly**

1. Click on an item from your list
2. Click **"Log New Exposure"**
3. **Before**: Rate anxiety before starting (pre-filled with current level)
4. **Do the exposure** - Face the feared situation
5. **During**: What was your peak anxiety during?
6. **After**: Rate anxiety after completing
7. **Duration**: How long (in minutes)
8. **Notes** (optional): What did you notice?
9. **Save** - Your distress chart updates

**Step 3: Track Habituation**

- **View progress chart**: Shows distress over time
  - Blue line = Before each exposure
  - Orange line = During (peak)
  - Green line = After
  - Dashed line = Your target
- **Notice the trend**: After line should decrease over multiple exposures
- **Mark complete**: When you reach your target distress level

**Tips for success**:
- Start with lower-anxiety items first
- Repeat each exposure 5-10 times before moving up
- Stay in the situation until anxiety decreases (don't escape at peak)
- Shorter, frequent exposures work better than one long exposure

## Mindfulness

### Meditation Timer

**Duration options**: 3, 5, 10, 15, or 20 minutes

**How to use**:
1. Go to Tools â†’ Mindfulness â†’ Timer
2. Choose duration
3. Tap **"Start"**
4. Close your eyes or lower your gaze
5. Focus on your breath
6. When mind wanders, gently return to breath
7. Bell sounds at intervals and at end

**Session saved automatically** with duration and timestamp

### Breath Awareness Practice

**Duration**: 5 minutes guided

**Purpose**: Learn to observe breathing without changing it

**Phases**:
1. **Settling**: Get comfortable, close eyes
2. **Observing**: Notice natural breath rhythm
3. **Counting**: Count breaths 1-10, then repeat
4. **Deepening**: Notice breath sensations in body
5. **Closing**: Gentle return to present awareness

**Great for beginners** - More structured than open meditation

### Body Scan Meditation

**Duration options**: 5 or 15 minutes

**Purpose**: Release tension by systematically relaxing body parts

**How it works**:
1. Lie down or sit comfortably
2. Follow voice guidance
3. Bring attention to each body part
4. Notice sensations without judgment
5. Release tension as you breathe

**Body parts**: Feet â†’ Legs â†’ Hips â†’ Back â†’ Chest â†’ Hands â†’ Arms â†’ Shoulders â†’ Neck â†’ Face â†’ Head

**Best for**: Anxiety, muscle tension, insomnia preparation

1. Morning: Check planned activities, log mood
2. Midday: Complete activities, use breathing if needed
